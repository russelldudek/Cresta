Actual mandate

Nominal

Lead complex technical or AI-driven SaaS implementations end to end.

Actual

Convert product velocity into repeatable enterprise value by aligning technical readiness, operating change, frontline adoption, evidence, and ownership from discovery through post-launch optimization.

What success appears to require

Customer outcome

  • Solutions launch and are used.
  • Success metrics are defined before go-live.
  • Customers finish deployment confident and ready to scale.

Internal operating value

  • Complex initiatives gain clarity and momentum.
  • Product, Engineering, Customer Success, and GTM receive useful signal.
  • Delivery learning becomes more reusable without erasing customer context.

Four activation conditions

01 / Outcome

Baseline, business metric, target, decision owner, and the operational meaning of “better.”

Failure: activity without business movement

02 / Readiness

Data, integration, policy, security, dependency, exception, and delivery conditions.

Failure: a green plan with red prerequisites

03 / Adoption

Frontline behavior, supervisor reinforcement, enablement, trust, feedback, and change burden.

Failure: availability mistaken for use

04 / Evidence

Evaluation, telemetry, quality threshold, business delta, intervention, and optimization signal.

Failure: go-live becomes the last measured event

Hard objection

“Russell's strongest evidence is deployment-shaped operating leadership—not a decade of contact-center SaaS implementation.”

That gap should be examined, not hidden. The adjacent evidence is technical-customer programs, operating launches, AI-enabled workflow transformation, telemetry, training, standard work, and governance. The value proposition is the ability to learn Cresta's product and delivery system quickly while bringing mature operating mechanics to the customer boundary.

No production CCaaS ownership is claimed.

What this candidate would not do

Prescribe early

Observe the existing system before redesigning it.

Confuse activity with impact

Connect milestones to adoption and customer metrics.

Hide the gap

Use evidence and learning posture instead of inflated claims.

bringing into the room.

Executive interview questions

  1. When Colin and the team call a deployment healthy, which signals matter before go-live—and which matter thirty days after it?
  2. Where do otherwise strong Cresta deployments most often lose momentum: customer decisions, data and integration readiness, frontline adoption, or post-launch ownership?
  3. How are responsibilities divided among AI Deployment, Forward-Deployed Engineering, Customer Success, Product, and the customer's operating team?
  4. As Conductor compresses build time, what new pressure does that create for customer discovery, decision-making, change management, or measurement?
  5. What does an excellent Deployment Manager make easier for the customer that the product cannot solve on its own?
  6. How does deployment learning currently travel back into product priorities, reusable playbooks, and commercial scoping?
  7. What is the hardest plausible concern about my background for this role, and what evidence would help you resolve it?
  8. Twelve months from now, what would make you say this hire materially improved how Cresta turns launches into scalable customer value?
