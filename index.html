The operating model

Four conditions cross together. Ownership keeps them together.

  1. 01 / Outcome

    Define the decision before configuring the solution.

    Baseline, business metric, target, decision owner, and the operational meaning of “better.”

    Failure signal: activity without business movement
  2. 02 / Readiness

    Make the hidden dependencies discussable.

    Data, integrations, policies, security, availability, exception paths, and delivery capacity.

    Failure signal: a green plan with red prerequisites
  3. 03 / Adoption

    Design how the work and reinforcement will change.

    Frontline behavior, supervisor cadence, enablement, trust, feedback, and change burden.

    Failure signal: availability mistaken for use
  4. 04 / Evidence

    Instrument the decision to release, intervene, or expand.

    Evaluation, telemetry, quality thresholds, business deltas, learning cadence, and optimization triggers.

    Failure signal: go-live becomes the last measured event
Binding layer Every condition needs a named owner, a decision moment, and evidence that travels.
