## 1. Campaign purpose

Build an independent candidate campaign that demonstrates how Russell would convert Cresta's AI capabilities into adopted customer workflows and measurable business outcomes. The campaign must make a coherent argument across the site, resume, cover letter, interview brief, entry plan, objection analysis, executive questions, and a role-specific deployment scorecard.

## 2. Actual mandate

The nominal responsibility is to lead technical or AI-driven SaaS deployments. The inferred mandate is broader:

> Convert product velocity into repeatable enterprise value by aligning technical readiness, operating change, frontline adoption, evidence, and ownership from discovery through post-launch optimization.

Cresta has publicly reported more than $100 million in annual recurring revenue and continued Fortune 500 expansion. ## 3. Central operating contradictions

The campaign will show tactical empathy for five tensions:

1. Fast deployment versus enterprise production rigor.
2. Reusable delivery practices versus customer-specific systems, policies, and workflows.
3. Automation ambition versus frontline, supervisor, and executive trust.
4. Technical go-live versus sustained adoption and measurable behavior change.
5. Project completion versus continuous testing, tuning, and value expansion.

## 5. Signature visual system: The Activation Boundary

### 5.1 Hero composition

The hero is a two-state field separated by a responsive activation boundary:

- Left state: `Capability shipped`
- Right state: `Outcome realized`
- Center: the four-condition activation boundary

Conversation-shaped signal packets approach the boundary. A packet crosses only when the scenario's outcome, readiness, adoption, and evidence conditions are strong enough. A weak condition causes the packet to stall, diffuse, or lose value before crossing. A successful crossing changes the downstream operating signal and reveals the recommended deployment intervention.

The hero must present the official Cresta logo above the fold, unmodified and correctly proportioned, beside the qualifier `Independent candidate vision by Russell Dudek`.

### 5.2 Activation conditions

The simulation exposes four parallel conditions rather than a generic sequential project rail:

- **Outcome:** baseline, target metric, decision owner, and definition of success.
- **Readiness:** data, integration, policy, security, dependency, and delivery conditions.
- **Adoption:** frontline behavior, supervisor reinforcement, training, trust, and change burden.
- **Evidence:** telemetry, evaluation, feedback cadence, intervention trigger, and optimization signal.

Ownership is the binding layer across all four conditions. It is not a fifth decorative gate.

### 5.3 Scenario interaction

The user can choose one of three illustrative deployment scenarios:

1. AI Agent
2. Agent Assist
3. Conversation Intelligence

Each scenario has an intentionally weak activation condition. Selecting or strengthening that condition changes:

- packet-throughput behavior;
- the stalled or realized operating state;
- a short deployment diagnosis;
- the next decision Russell would facilitate;
- the customer-facing success signal to validate.

The scenarios are hypotheses for demonstrating the operating model. They will not be presented as inside knowledge of Cresta's proprietary implementation process.

### 5.4 Motion contract

Motion must explain state change:

- packets move toward the activation boundary;
- weak conditions visibly absorb or scatter the packet;
- strong conditions allow the packet to cross;
- the downstream signal stabilizes only after evidence and ownership are established;
- scenario selection immediately recomputes the visual state and diagnosis.

Hover effects and generic scroll reveals do not satisfy the interaction contract. With `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, packets become static before/after markers, all diagnoses remain visible, and no information depends on animation.

## 7. Information architecture

The site will use a non-card-stack narrative:

1. **Hero / Activation Boundary:** thesis and guided interaction.
2. **The company moment:** growth, product velocity, and why deployment becomes the constraint.
3. **What customers are actually asking the deployment team to absorb:** the five operating tensions.
4. **Activation model:** the four conditions, ownership layer, and decision logic.
5. **Evidence trail:** an annotated career timeline mapping verified experience to deployment work.
6. **First 90 days:** a three-horizon entry plan focused on learning, proving, and making delivery more reusable.
7. **Hard objection:** an explicit response to Russell's lack of conventional CCaaS implementation tenure.
8. **Interview workspace:** executive questions, downloadable brief, scorecard, resume, and cover letter.

The full page must maintain deliberate pacing and art direction. Repeated four-card grids, dark objection bands, floating nodes, orbit rings, dispatch queues, stage rails, and control-room layouts are prohibited because they collide with prior campaign fingerprints.

## 11. Technical architecture

Use a static, dependency-light architecture suitable for GitHub Pages:

- semantic HTML routes;
- shared CSS tokens and print modules;
- vanilla JavaScript modules for simulation, navigation, and accessibility;
- inline or locally committed SVG for original diagrams;
- locally committed official brand assets;
- no runtime API dependency;
- deterministic PDF generation from print routes using a headless browser;
- automated page-count, link, text, metadata, overflow, and forbidden-name checks.

The simulation state will be a small immutable scenario map. UI controls will update ARIA state, diagnosis text, CSS custom properties, and SVG classes from that map. Invalid or missing state will fall back to the first scenario and expose a readable static diagnosis.

## 12. Accessibility and responsive behavior

- WCAG-aware contrast and focus visibility;
- semantic landmarks and heading order;
- full keyboard operation for scenario and condition controls;
- descriptive labels for the visual model;
- live-region updates for scenario diagnosis without excessive announcements;
- no information conveyed by color alone;
- complete reduced-motion treatment;
- responsive views validated at desktop, laptop, tablet, and mobile widths;
- document routes reflow on small screens while print retains exact US Letter geometry.
